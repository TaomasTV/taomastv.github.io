About me

Ever since I started studying Game Design at HKU, I’ve been drawn to the challenge of turning ideas into playable experiences. I enjoy exploring how systems, mechanics, and player feedback come together to create meaning and fun.

Most of my projects focus on experimentation, whether that’s designing surreal exploration games, prototyping unique recipe systems, or building small mechanics that test how players react. I see every prototype as a chance to learn, improve, and discover new ways of engaging players.

My long-term ambition, besides starting my own indie Game Studio, is to work on competitive, multiplayer-focused titles like Valorant, where strong design directly impacts the way players strategize, communicate, and experience the game. I thrive in collaborative environments and love iterating based on feedback.

Projects I am currently working on

You're Not Welcome Here

A short narrative-focused horror exploration game.

Step into a surreal environment where every space feels familiar yet wrong. As you explore, the world reacts to your presence in unsettling ways, blurring the line between story and atmosphere. Designed to immerse players in tension and unease, the game focuses on environmental storytelling and psychological horror rather than jumpscares.
